What is Curbox?

Curbox is a screen-time and app-blocker for Android built for people who want their attention back without handing their data to anyone. It uses Android's Accessibility Service to watch for the apps and websites you choose and show a configurable pause screen when you reach a limit. The app ships under the GNU GPL v3 license, has no internet permission, and is built in the open by its community.

It is organised into three areas: Usage (see where your time goes), Focus (timed deep-work sessions that block distractions), and Reducers (a toolbox of limits and gentle nudges).

What you can do

Granular blocking

Block a whole app, a single feature like Instagram Reels or YouTube Shorts, or one URL path while leaving the rest usable.

Smart rules & schedules

Set usage limits, time windows, and unlock caps so a block applies only when you actually need it.

Unlock challenges

Choose your friction in advance: a cooldown, a QR or barcode scan, or typing out your intent before an app opens.

Focus mode

Pause distractions on demand for deep work and watch your focused time add up across sessions.

Insights & live awareness

See per-app and per-website usage, plus a live count of how much short-form content you have scrolled today.

Mindful nudges

Per-app grayscale, auto Do Not Disturb, and reflection messages that quietly tilt your habits the right way.

See the full feature breakdown for every option in depth.

Privacy first, fully on-device

Curbox has no internet permission, no telemetry, no accounts, and no data collection. Your usage analytics never leave your phone, and because the entire codebase is open source, that promise is verifiable rather than just stated.

Get Curbox

Curbox is free and always will be. Install it from either source:

  • F-Droid for automatic updates through the open-source app store
  • The latest GitHub release for a direct APK download

New to the app? Read the documentation to set up your first block, or learn more about why Curbox exists.
